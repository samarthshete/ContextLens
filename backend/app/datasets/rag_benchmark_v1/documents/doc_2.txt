Embedding models convert text into numerical vectors. The quality of embeddings significantly impacts retrieval performance.

Chunking refers to splitting documents into smaller segments before embedding. Smaller chunks increase retrieval precision but risk losing context. Larger chunks preserve context but may reduce relevance due to noise.

Choosing the right chunk size is critical. If chunks are too small, the system may retrieve incomplete information. If chunks are too large, the retrieval system may return irrelevant sections.
